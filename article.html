DENNISE'S 5 BENEFITS OF READING BOOKS: WHY YOU SHOULD READ EVERY DAY

There are so many benefits to reading books. But let's face it: It can be challenging to motivate ourselves to read a 382-page book when we can watch the movie, listen to the audiobook, or watch a YouTube video summary instead. However, if most of your daily reading consists of social media posts and texts, you're missing out.

So, what are the benefits of reading books?

If you're interested in reading more books but need some motivation, this article's for you. After all, when you understand the importance of reading books, you're more likely to do it. Here are 10 benefits of reading that illustrate the importance of reading books:

(1) Gain Valuable Knowledge, one of the most obvious benefits of reading every day is learning. Unlike a YouTube video or a podcast, books provide access to in-depth knowledge. In other words, if you want to become more productive, absorb more information, and change your habits, you are in need of books! Just like the author Roald Dahl once said, "If you are going to get anywhere in life, you have to read a lot of books."

(2) Exercise Your Brain, as the 17th century English writer Joseph Addison once wrote, "Reading is to the mind, what exercise is to the body." Which means, reading gives your mind exercise just as you exercise for your body. Research has confirmed that reading stimulates a complex network of circuits and signals in the brain. Plus, as you improve your reading ability, these networks become stronger and more sophisticated. In another study, researchers measured how reading a novel affects our brains. The study's participants read the novel "Pompeii" by Robert Harris, and as tension in the story developed, more areas of the brain were activated. And the best part is, the scans showed that brain connectivity increased while reading every day. Bottom line, our brains, just like our muscles, if we don't exercise them regularly, our cognitive abilities may decline. However, when we read every day, we can keep them strong and healthy.

(3) Improve Focus, one of the key benefits of reading is that it helps you practice concentrating on just one thing at a time. For example, a novel requires all of your attention for it to pull you in to its world. And if you want to learn from a non-fiction book, it requires you to be fully present and engaged. In short, if you want to succeed, you need to focus. And if you want to focus more, you can practice by reading books.

(4) Improve Memory, one of the advantages of reading books is that it can improve your memory. When you read a non-fiction book, you also consume an enormous amount of information on the subject you're reading about. Similarly, whenever you read a novel, you have to remember tons of information about the story's plot, the characters, their relationships, and the environment in which the story takes place, which is a lot of information! All of these new information creates new memories. And every new memory strengthens the old ones. Reading every day can improve your memory, help you learn how to store new information, and recall memories more effectively.

(5) Improve Communication Skills. The ability to communicate effectively is a vital life skill. In fact, one study discovered that 69% of employers are looking to hire people with "soft" skills, such as effective communication. One of the key benefits of reading is that it helps us to communicate better. How? Reading every day can improve our communication skills in a few ways. For example, reading can influence your writing and increase your vocabulary. When we read well-written work, we naturally observe its writing style and composition. These characteristics unconsciously and automatically applies to your writing, in the same way that musicians are influenced by each other. What's more, studies show that those who read regularly tend to develop large vocabularies. Finally, don't forget that reading also help improve our communication skills by increasing our ability to empathize and understand others. Bottom line, read more, communicate better, and improve!
